Exploring Why Catholicism Is True

This site is an educational hub designed to help you explore the most fundamental theological differences between Catholicism and Protestant Christianity. Each doctrinal topic has its own page with a concise summary of what the issue is, why it matters, a doctrinal survey from both sides, strong arguments for the Protestant position, and a robust Catholic response rooted in Scripture, the early Church, and the teaching authority (Magisterium) of the Church. You will also find recommended articles, videos and primary sources for further study.

Topics Covered

  • Epistemology: Scripture, Tradition & Authority
  • Justification: Faith, Works & Salvation
  • Sacramentology: Signs, Grace & Efficacy
  • Ecclesiology: The Nature of the Church
  • Authority & Unity: Fathers, Councils & Development

Catholics and Protestants share a love for Jesus Christ and the Scriptures, but they diverge on how divine revelation is received and interpreted, how salvation works, how many sacraments Christ instituted, what the Church is, and who has authority to settle doctrinal questions. Our goal is to explore these questions charitably and in depth.

For ease of navigation, use the menu at the top of each page to move between topics. Footnotes provide links to authoritative sources where direct quotations are used.
